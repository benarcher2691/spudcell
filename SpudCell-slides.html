SpudCell

The first bottom-up synthetic cell shown to run a complete cell cycle — feed, grow, replicate, divide — from chemically defined, non-living parts.

Kate Adamala Lab · University of Minnesota Twin Cities · Preprint · 1 July 2026

What it is

A cell built from scratch — every part known

  • Fatty membrane enclosing a defined genome + cell-free (PURE) expression system
  • Built bottom-up — vs. Venter's 2010 top-down stripped bacterium
  • Named to echo Sputnik; Adamala: "I'm Polish… mostly made of potatoes"
~90kbgenome across 7 plasmids
~36genes (E. coli + viral)
Genome size — SpudCell is ~50× smaller than a bacterium

The goal

Endy: build "an operating system for life" and coordinate the field

Demonstrated

A complete cell cycle — coupled for the first time

StepMechanism
Feed / growαHL displays His-tag → Ni-NTA feeder vesicles fuse in
ReplicatePhi29 replicase, rolling-circle (TTcDR)
DivideFLAG-tag + streptavidin repulsion splits droplet
SelectGrowth mutation outcompetes originals

The long-standing hurdle: coupling growth & division to gene expression — each needs different conditions.

How it feeds

Genetically encoded fusion

A protein the cell expresses drives the fusion — so growth is written in the genome, coupling metabolism to fitness.

Capabilities vs limits

Real — but not alive

✓ It can

  • ✓Feed & grow via genetically encoded fusion
  • ✓Replicate its genome
  • ✓Divide (tag-driven or by extrusion)
  • ✓Undergo selection — a fitter mutant outcompeted originals

✕ It cannot

  • ✕Make its own ribosomes (they degrade)
  • ✕Regulate metabolism or clear waste
  • ✕Divide cleanly — only ~30% keep full genome after 5 cycles
  • ✕Live independently — "conks out" after a few generations

Adamala: "Proof of principle that molecules can reconstitute behaviours we only associated with living cells."
